General

  1. What is manufactured housing?

    A manufactured home is built in a factory, according to the Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards of June 1976 (“the HUD Code”-- 24 C.F.R. Part 3280), and secured on a permanent, nonremovable steel frame or chassis. The home can be built as one complete section, or in multiple sections, that are transported, assembled and installed at the home site.

  2. How is manufactured housing different from a mobile or modular home?

    Mobile homes, or trailers, are built on wheels and can be pulled by a vehicle. Built prior to the 1976 HUD Code, these structures are defined as “mobile homes’, and mortgages secured by mobile homes are not eligible for sale to Freddie Mac.

    Manufactured homes must be built in compliance with the the HUD Code The towing hitch, wheels, and axles must be removed and the dwelling must assume the characteristics of site-built housing. Like manufactured homes, modular homes (or other factory built dwellings)  are not built to compliance with the HUD code. They’re built to conform to  state, local or regional building codes and are usually legally classified as real property. Typically, modular or other factory built homes (not HUD Code homes) are  eligible  as site-built single-family home or financing, appraisal and construction financing purposes.

  3. What is a "site-built" home?

    Traditional "site-built" homes are constructed entirely at the building site, not a factory. Common construction materials are 2x4s and 4x6s precut wood used for framing and trusses. Site-built homes must conform to state, local or regional codes where the house is located and they comprise the majority of all new homes constructed today.

  4. How do I know a home meets the HUD Code?

    When newly built, every manufactured home is inspected at the factory by an independent third-party inspector certified by HUD. The manufactured home will have:

    A special HUD Certification Label affixed to the exterior of the home indicating it has been designed, constructed and inspected to comply with the HUD Code standards

    A HUD Data Plate/Compliance Certificate, which is a paper label mounted in the home, that contains, among other things, the manufacturer's name, trade/model name, year manufactured and serial number, and a list of the certification label number(s), etc. The data plate is typically affixed in a readily accessible and visible location (e.g., near the main electrical panel, in a kitchen cabinet, or a bedroom closet).

  5. What is the difference between a manufactured home titled as real property and a manufactured home titled as personal property?

    Manufactured homes may be legally classified as real property or personal property based on how they are titled in accordance with state law. In general, manufactured homes can only be classified as real property if they are permanently affixed to the land. A loan secured by a manufactured home titled as real property will be secured by the manufactured home itself and the real property (land) the manufactured home is located on. Freddie Mac purchases mortgages secured by manufactured homes titled as real property.

    Manufactured homes classified as personal property are generally treated more like a vehicle under state law. Many times, the owner owns the manufactured home and rents or leases the real property on which the manufactured home is located. A loan secured by a manufactured home that is titled as personal property will generally be secured by the manufactured home, but not the land the manufactured home is located on. Freddie Mac does not currently purchase mortgages secured by manufactured homes titled as personal property. However, we’re exploring opportunities to purchase this type of financing.

  6. What are Freddie Mac's proposed Single-Family Duty to Serve activities in support of manufactured housing?

    Through our Duty to Serve Underserved Markets plan, we will

    • Purchase more loans used to finance manufactured homes titled as real property,
    • Develop new sustainable capabilities to buy and securitize personal property (chattel) loans.
    • Enhance current products and create new offerings
    • Conduct a personal property (chattel) test and learn pilot program to better understand the market
      for manufactured homes titled as personal property, or chattel, and to support purchases of loans
      secured for these homes.
    • Provide consumer education to help borrowers navigate both the real property and personal property markets.
  7. What is Freddie Mac doing to provide consumer education for potential borrowers interested in manufactured housing?

    We’re leveraging relationships with local organizations in certain states to broaden access to homebuyer education and housing counseling for individuals and families with very low-, low- and moderate-incomes. Specifically, we’re conducting a “test and learn” pilot with, Next Step® Network, a  Louisville-based nonprofit company housing intermediary to focus on and increase lender participation in the manufactured housing marketplace. Through Next Step’s SmartMHSM manufactured housing homebuyer education program, lenders will engage with prospective homebuyers who want to purchase a manufactured home by helping them navigate to a HUD-certified, nonprofit housing counseling organization that provides information about financing options, the benefits of energy-efficient features and  access to homebuyer education. and credit-rebuilding services.

Mortgage Eligibility and Underwriting

  1. What products does Freddie Mac currently offer to help finance a manufactured home?

    Freddie Mac eligible mortgage products for manufactured homes include 15-, 20- and 30-year fixed-rate mortgages; 7/1 and 10/1 adjustable rate mortgages (ARMs); integrated construction conversion documentation or single close; Home Possible Mortgages® may be secured by manufactured homes, subject to conditions.

  2. What is Freddie doing to assist financially challenged borrowers in underserved markets with a down payment and closing costs for a manufactured home?

    We’re assessing our policies for down payment requirements and options for down payment assistance to help borrowers who are purchasing a manufactured home and titling it as real property.

    We intend to work with lenders and nonprofits to give borrowers responsible down payment assistance options and increase the ability of homebuyers with very low-, low- and moderate-income to purchase a manufactured home titled as real property.

  3. Can a borrower use sweat equity toward down payment and closing costs?

    Yes. Our current policy allows for sweat equity as a source of funds on Home Possible® loans only; however, under the current policy borrowers are required to contribute 5 percent from personal funds toward some of their down payment before they can use any sweat equity credit. We’re reviewing our current Single Family Seller Servicer Guide  so that borrowers will be able to apply sweat equity to cover some or all of their down payment and closing costs.

  4. May a borrower use gift funds or grant money toward a down payment for a manufactured home loan?

    Yes. Currently, our Single-Family Seller/Servicer Guide allows the use of gift and/or grant money that the borrower does not have to repay.

  5. Are manufactured housing loans eligible for automated collateral evaluation ACE)?

    No. Currently loans secured by manufactured homes are not eligible for an ACE waiver.

  7. What are the requirements for submitting a manufactured housing loan to Loan Product Advisor®

    Manufactured homes titled as real property must be  evaluated by Loan Product Advisor® to be eligible for sale to Freddie Mac.

CHOICEHomeSM

  1. What is CHOICEHomeSM?

    CHOICEHome are is a new type of high-quality, energy-efficient, real property factory-built home, built to the HUD code, with features comparable to site-built homes, including, drywall throughout higher-pitch roof line, energy-efficient features, lower profile foundation plus additional options such as a garage or carport.

  2. When will CHOICEHome financing be available broadly?

    Before CHOICEHome is launched broadly, Freddie Mac will conduct a three-year pilot to bring conventional loan financing to this type of factory-built home. We’ll test all aspects of the loan origination process to see what works and what doesn’t, so we bring valuable financing terms to market.

  3. How can I participate as a Seller of CHOICEHomes to Freddie Mac?

    We have a limited number of customers experienced in manufactured housing lending participating in the initial phase of the pilot.  As industry production of these homes begins to ramp up, we will consider adding customers to the pilot, potentially as soon as the third quarter of 2019.
