Supporting Middle Appalachia, Lower Mississippi Delta and the Colonias


Middle Appalachia

Kentucky, North Caroline, Ohio, Tennessee, Virginia, West Virginia

Demographic: Aging population

Key housing challenges:

  • Outmigration with younger workforce
  • Distressed properties that need to be renovated
  • Limited access to financial and homebuying education
  • Common for land to be passed from one
    generation the next

Lower Mississippi Delta

Mississippi, Louisiana, Arkansas, Alabama, Tennessee, Kentucky, Missouri, Illinois

Demographic: Poorest populations in the country

Key housing challenges:

  • Suffered through many natural disasters – destroyed properties
  • Distressed properties that need to be renovated
  • Limited access to financial and homebuying education
  • High-degree of out-migration with younger workforce

Colonias

Arizona, California, New Mexico, Texas (along US-Mexico border)

Demographic: foreign-born residents;

Key housing challenges:

  • Mostly language barriers
  • Challenging appraisal process
  • Distressed properties that need to be renovated
  • Limited access to financial and homebuying education
  • Some residents exposed to contract-for-deed abuse

Here’s what we’re doing in these high needs rural regions

  • Increasing future homebuyer access to education and resources. Homebuyer education is critical to developing more informed, mortgage-ready borrowers and is key to responsible, sustainable homeownership. We are implementing homebuyer and consumer credit education programs in rural markets in high-needs areas with our strategic alliances with FAHE, Hope Enterprise Corporation and the Community Development Corporation of Brownsville. These organizations bring specific local expertise and insights as we work together to develop affordable housing education and access programs in these regions.
  • Purchasing more single-family loans.
  • Designing the following:
    • New product flexibilities to facilitate the origination of mortgages
    • Improved product flexibilities to facilitate the origination of renovation mortgages
  • Purchasing more loans from small financial institutions serving rural regions

Duty to Serve rural populations: Supporting Native American Tribes

Native American Tribes

326 Native American reservations; 23 percent of Native Americans live on native American land

Demographic:

Key housing challenges:

  • Legal complexities of land titles make mortgage financing difficult
  • Limited access to financial and homebuying education
  • Substandard housing

Here’s what we’re doing in these rural populations

  • Increasing homebuyer access to education and resources for members of a Federally Recognized Indian Tribe in Indian areas. This is critical to developing more informed, mortgage-ready borrowers and is key to responsible, sustainable homeownership. We’re collaborating with South Dakota’s Homebuyer Education Resource Organization (HERO) to provide resources, housing counseling certifications and homebuyer education to Native American organizations supporting tribal areas in South Dakota. Freddie Mac will work jointly with HERO to train and certify homebuyer education counselors and expand these resources to other tribal areas.
  • Increasing technical expertise in Indian areas
  • Developing a robust renovation mortgage product to help rehabilitate aging houses.

 


Lenders and borrowers with low, very low and moderate income face the following challenges in rural markets in high-needs areas – limited access to financial services, appraisal challenges, aging housing stock and borrowers with limited knowledge about the homebuying process. Here’s how we’re addressing these challenges.

  • Improving the distribution of investment capital and bring greater liquidity to small financial institutions
  • Leading lender training sessions
  • Providing guidance on appraisal guidelines for rural homes We’re adding more appraisal data on rural properties to Home Value Explorer® (HVE®), our automated valuation model that helps generate property value estimates.
  • Enhancing our product offerings
  • Implementing homebuyer and consumer credit education programs in rural markets in high-needs areas with our strategic alliances with Federation of Appalachian Housing Enterprises (FAHE), Hope Enterprise Corporation and the Community Development Corporation of Brownsville (CDCB).
  • Partnering with NextJob to provide re-employment services to
    • Our strategic nonprofit partners to assist their clients become mortgage ready
    • Servicers of Home Possible® loans for borrowers who experience unemployment and are located in a Duty to Serve high-needs area
  • Collaborate with the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) and the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) to identify and promote more affordable housing options

Partnership Profile

Native American Homebuyer Outreach with South Dakota’s Homebuyer Education Resource Organization (HERO)

Freddie Mac, in collaboration with the South Dakota Native Homeownership Coalition and Seven Sisters Community Development Group, LLC, has partnered with South Dakota’s Homeownership Education Resource Organization (HERO) to help put aspiring Native American homeowners on the path to homeownership. As a first step, Freddie Mac is working with HERO to train and certify homebuyer education counselors. The training and certification component is a critical area to homebuyer education since the lack of counselors’ training and certification was recognized as a barrier to the 16 Native housing organizations identified as potential HERO Partners. Once certified, these counselors will be able to provide homebuyer education and financial literacy to Native Americans in the South Dakotan Indian areas.
