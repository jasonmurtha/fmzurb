Real Property Manufactured Housing

DEFINITION: Real Property Manufactured Housing: These are homes where the borrower finances and owns both the land and home, and it meets all applicable real property requirements. Freddie Mac currently support financing for this type of manufactured home.


We currently support financing for real property manufactured homes, but we want to do more. Through Duty to Serve, we’re working with industry stakeholders to:

  • Purchase more loans used to finance manufactured homes titled as real property
  • Enhance current products and create new offerings
  • Provide consumer education to help borrowers navigate the real property market
  • Expand our affordable housing vision by providing conventional loan financing with CHOICEHomeSM, a new type of manufactured home

Personal Property Manufactured Housing (Chattel)

DEFINITION: Personal Property Manufactured Housing (Chattel): A manufactured home titled as personal property or chattel is when the loan used to purchase the home or refinance the home uses only the home as collateral and not the land where the home is sited. The security lien for the loan is placed on the title to the home. The homeowner may own the land, rent or lease the land on which the home is located.


We’re currently exploring opportunities to purchase personal property or chattel loans.

Through Duty to Serve, we’re working with industry stakeholders to:

  • Develop new sustainable capabilities to buy and securitize personal property or chattel loans.
  • Conducting a personal property (chattel) test and learn pilot program to better understand the market for manufactured homes titled as personal property, or chattel, and to support purchases of loans secured for these homes.
  • Provide consumer education to help borrowers navigate both the personal property market.
